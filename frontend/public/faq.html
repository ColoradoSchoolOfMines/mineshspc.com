Frequently Asked Questions

How many people can be on my team?

We recommend a team size of three, but will allow teams of two or four. Teams of more than four students are not allowed, nor is it allowed to compete individually.

What languages can we use?

You can use C, C++, Python, Java, JavaScript, or Ruby. We recommend that everyone on your team know at least one of these languages in common.

What editors do the competition PCs have?

The competition PCs run on Linux (Distribution: Ubuntu latest) and have Visual Studio Code, PyCharm, Eclipse, and IntelliJ IDEA.

How long is the actual competition?

Kattis will be open for 4 hours, from 10:00am-2:00pm Mountain Time. There are announcements for an hour at the start and end of the competition.

Can I bring my own laptop or tablet?

Yes! As per the rules, teams are only allowed to use one competition PC. We find that this benefits team problem solving ability.

That being said, participants are welcome to bring their own laptops and tablets if they wish. There are no software requirements for laptops or tablets, only that you can sign into Kattis to submit problems.
